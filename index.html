Iris Christadler

Computational Specialist — LMU Geophysics, Munich

Iris Christadler received her diploma in computer science from the University of Erlangen-Nuremberg in 2004. Her thesis on multi-grid methods for calculating optical flow with non-standard regularizers was supervised by Harald Köstler (Chair of System Simulation, Prof. Rüde). She worked in the High Performance Software Group of the Leibniz Supercomputing Centre in Munich from 2005 to 2012, serving as task and work package leader in PRACE and PRACE-1IP, with a focus on novel architectures and new parallel programming languages. After parental leave, she joined the computational seismology team of Prof. Alice-Agnes Gabriel at LMU in 2022. She now leads the tasks "Software as a Service" and "Technical Training" in the EU-funded project Geo-INQUIRE, with a focus on Research Software Data Management and the use of Simulation Data Lakes to enable FAIR sharing of simulation results.
